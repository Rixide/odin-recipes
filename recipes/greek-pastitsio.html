Greek Pastitsio Recipe

Description

Pastitsio is often called the "Greek lasagna". Instead of flat sheets, it features a base of tubular pasta mixed with egg and cheese, a middle layer of spiced beef sweet with hints of cinnamon, and a thick, velvety top layer of baked béchamel sauce.

ingredients

  • The Pasta Base: 12 oz bucatini or penne pasta, 2 egg whites, 1 cup crumbled feta cheese.
  • The Meat Sauce: 1.5 lbs ground beef, 1 diced yellow onion, 2 minced garlic cloves, 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes, 2 tbsp tomato paste, ½ cup red wine, 1 tsp ground cinnamon, ½ tsp ground allspice, salt, and pepper.
  • The Béchamel Sauce: ¼ cup butter, ¼ cup flour, 2.5 cups warm milk, ¼ tsp nutmeg, 2 egg yolks, 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese.

Steps

  1. Cook Meat: Sauté onions and garlic in olive oil. Add beef and cook until browned
  2. Simmer Sauce: Pour in wine, tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, cinnamon, and allspice. Simmer for 20 minutes until thick.
  3. Boil Pasta: Cook pasta to al dente. Drain, let cool slightly, then stir in egg whites and crumbled feta.
  4. Make Béchamel: Melt butter in a pan, whisk in flour for 1 minute, then slowly whisk in warm milk until thick. Remove from heat; whisk in nutmeg, egg yolks, and half the Parmesan.
  5. Assemble: Grease a 9x13-inch pan. Layer all the pasta at the bottom, spread the meat sauce evenly over it, and pour the béchamel smoothly on top. Sprinkle with the remaining Parmesan.
  6. Bake: Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 45 minutes until the top is deeply golden brown. Rest 20 minutes before slicing.
